Application Guide

Simple to Apply.
Perfect Every Time.

Follow these steps exactly as printed on your Parmafix bag for a professional, crack-free finish every job.

01
🧱

Surface Preparation

Clean the wall — remove dust, oil, loose paint, and contaminants. Patch holes. Ensure substrate is structurally sound.

💡 Apply primer if the surface is glossy or dusty — improves adhesion dramatically.
02
🪣

Mixing

Pour clean water into your mixing bucket first. Slowly add Parmafix powder (2 parts powder : 1 part water). Mix with a power drill and paddle until no lumps — smooth and consistent.

💡 Let sit 5 minutes then mix again (slaking). Only mix what you can use in 30–40 minutes — it sets fast once activated.
03
🔧

Application

Use a wide knife to spread a thin, even 2–4 mm layer. Work from top to bottom in sweeping motions. Apply second or third coat if needed for a perfect finish.

💡 Don't overwork the surface while wet — apply evenly and leave it to set before the next coat.
04
⏱️

Setting & Drying

Allow 30–40 minutes initial set. Full drying takes 24–48 hours depending on temperature, humidity, and coat thickness.

💡 Avoid direct sun or strong wind during drying — can cause surface cracks. Cover openings if necessary.
05
🪚

Sanding

Once fully dry, smooth out imperfections with light sanding. Use 120-grit sandpaper for best results before the final finish coat.

💡 Repeat coating if needed — apply a second or third coat for a perfect finish.
06
🎨

Prime & Paint

Prime again before painting — this ensures the paint sticks properly. Paint only after full drying (24–48 hrs) for a flawless, long-lasting result.

💡 Parmafix's bright white finish reduces the number of paint coats needed — saving you money.

Does Parmafix crack after drying?+
No. Parmafix contains a powder polymer specifically formulated to resist hairline cracking — even in Kenya's heat and humidity changes. With proper surface preparation and the correct mixing ratio (2:1), cracking is not a concern.
What is the correct mixing ratio?+
The ratio is 2 parts Parmafix powder to 1 part clean water. Pour water into the bucket first, then add powder slowly while mixing. Use a power drill and paddle until completely smooth with no lumps. Let sit for 5 minutes, mix again (slaking), then apply.
How much coverage do I get per bag?+
One 25 kg bag covers 18–22 m² at a 2–4 mm application thickness. Coverage varies slightly depending on surface texture and technique. Rougher surfaces require more material.
How long before I can paint over Parmafix?+
Allow a minimum of 24–48 hours for full drying before painting. Prime again before painting to ensure the paint sticks properly. Never paint on a surface that hasn't fully dried.
Can it be used on exterior walls?+
Yes. Parmafix Premium Skimming Coat is specifically designed for both interior and exterior use. Its powder polymer formulation provides excellent weather resistance and durability for exterior surfaces.
What surfaces is Parmafix suitable for?+
Parmafix works on concrete walls, sand cement plaster, blocks, and brick — interior and exterior. The surface must be clean, dry, and structurally sound. Dampen slightly before applying to improve adhesion.
